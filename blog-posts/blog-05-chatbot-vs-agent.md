## The Playbook: Upgrading From Chatbot to AI Agent

If you're currently running a chatbot and wondering whether the upgrade is worth it, here's the honest framework:

### When a chatbot is still fine:

- Your site traffic is low (<500 visitors/month)
- Your product is self-serve with no sales team
- You only need FAQ deflection, not lead conversion
- Your average deal size doesn't justify the investment

### When you need an AI agent:

- You're spending real money on lead generation
- Your team can't respond to leads fast enough
- You have after-hours coverage gaps
- Your deal sizes justify personalized engagement
- You need voice + text + email, not just website chat
- You want meetings booked, not just emails collected

### The Migration Path

**Week 1: Audit your current chatbot performance.**
How many conversations does it have per month? How many of those result in a meeting booked? How many get "stuck" at the handoff to a human who isn't available? This baseline will tell you exactly how much pipeline you're leaving on the table.

**Week 2: Build your AI agent alongside the chatbot.**
Don't rip and replace. Deploy the AI agent on your highest-intent pages (pricing, demo request, contact) and keep the chatbot on lower-intent pages (blog, resources). Compare performance side by side.

**Week 3: Expand to voice and email.**
Once the text/chat agent is performing, add the phone number and email follow-up capabilities. Now you're covering channels your chatbot never could.

**Week 4: Evaluate and consolidate.**
If the AI agent is outperforming the chatbot (it will), transition fully. Keep the chatbot for FAQ deflection if needed, but route all buying-intent conversations through the agent.
