## The 4-Step Framework

### Step 1: Map Your Existing Playbook

Before you touch any tool, write down exactly what your best SDR does when a new lead comes in. Not what the process document says — what actually happens.

For most teams, it looks something like this:

- Lead fills out a form
- SDR gets a notification (eventually)
- SDR looks up the company on LinkedIn
- SDR checks if they match ICP criteria
- SDR sends a personalized email or makes a call
- If they connect: qualify → book a meeting
- If they don't: add to a sequence

**Your AI agent is going to do this exact workflow.** Not some futuristic sci-fi version of it. The same steps, the same questions, the same qualification criteria. Just faster and without the 42-hour gap.

> **Write it down. All of it. Your playbook is your agent's brain.**

---

### Step 2: Define Your Qualification Criteria

This is where most people rush and regret it later. Your agent needs clear rules for what makes a lead qualified. Think of it as programming an if/then tree — but in plain English.

**Example qualification framework:**

| Question | Qualified | Not Qualified |
|---|---|---|
| Company size | 50–500 employees | <10 employees |
| Role | VP, Director, C-suite | Intern, Student |
| Timeline | Active project, <6 months | "Just researching" |
| Budget | Has budget or can influence | No budget authority |

> **Pro tip:** Start with 3–5 qualification questions max. You can always add complexity later. Over-engineering your first agent is the fastest way to kill the project.

---

### Step 3: Build the Agent (The Actual Afternoon Part)

With a no-code platform, building the agent looks like this:

**Hour 1: Set up the agent profile.**
Give it a name, a personality, and guardrails. This isn't a chatbot — it's a representative of your brand. Define the tone (professional but warm), the boundaries (what it can and can't promise), and the escalation path (when to hand off to a human).

**Hour 2: Build the conversation flow.**
Using a drag-and-drop builder, map out the conversation your agent will have. Start with a greeting, move through qualification questions, handle common objections, and end with a clear next step (book a meeting, transfer to sales, send a resource).

**Hour 3: Connect your integrations.**
Plug in your CRM so leads get logged automatically. Connect your calendar so meetings get booked in real time. Set up notifications so your sales team knows the moment a qualified lead is ready.

**Hour 4: Test and refine.**
Run through the conversation yourself. Try to break it. Ask weird questions. See how it handles "I'm not interested" vs. "Tell me more about pricing." Adjust until it feels right.

> **Stat: Companies using no-code AI tools report 40% faster time-to-deployment compared to custom-built solutions.**

### Step 4: Deploy on Your Highest-Intent Pages

Don't put your new agent everywhere on day one. Start where it matters most:

1. **"Request a Demo" confirmation page** — They just asked for a demo. Your agent confirms, qualifies, and books it right there.
2. **Pricing page** — Visitors here are evaluating. Your agent answers questions and captures intent.
3. **After-hours inbound** — Any lead that comes in outside business hours gets an instant response instead of a 14-hour wait.

## The Three Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1: Making the agent sound like a robot.**
Nobody wants to talk to something that says "I am an AI assistant and I am here to help you today." Give it personality. Make it sound like your brand. If your brand is casual, the agent should be casual. If you're enterprise, keep it buttoned up.

**Mistake 2: Trying to handle every scenario on day one.**
Your first agent should handle the top 3–5 conversation paths. Edge cases can be routed to a human. You'll learn from real conversations and improve over time.

**Mistake 3: Not telling your sales team.**
Your SDRs need to know the agent exists, what it's doing, and how qualified leads will show up in their workflow. The worst outcome is a rep calling a lead who just had a great conversation with your agent and saying, "So, tell me about your company." Alignment matters.
